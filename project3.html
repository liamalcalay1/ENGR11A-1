Project 3

Alarm Clock

Project Description:

The goal of this project was for us to learn how to solder, further develop our Fusion skills, and to introduce us to laser cutting.

Soldering

I've soldered once before when I was still in middle school. I didn't find soldering too difficult especially as I got further in. However, I should have paid more attention to the correct alignments of certain pieces on the board as I oriented a few of them wrong. I did get some practice with the desoldering tool, which I found surprisingly fun. Overall, I enjoyed soldering and will definitely pay more attention to the orientations of the electronic pieces!

Board and Battery Pack Measurements and Sketch

My partner and I used calipers to measure the various measurements of the board and battery pack. We made sure to measure with the batteries in the pack. We decided to make a simple box with cutouts for the display and buttons.

Box in Fusion

Using Penny's in class tutorial and some help from Youtube, we were able to make a simple box. Teamwork was an aspect of this project that differentiated it from the previous two. Divvying up the work was a bit difficult, especially on Fusion, but we were able to work together to make the box.

First Iteration of 3D print

Our box ended up being too small as we didn't account for the thickness and expansion of the PLA. Additionally, the clearence we left for our sliding top was not enough.

Second Sketch of Box and Battery Pack

This sketch included dimensions for where the cutout for the buttons should be. Additionally, this sketch included more accurate dimensions.

Second Iteration of Box in Fusion

Second Iteration of Box in Prusaslicer

The print did not require supports. The only action we needed to do was to separate the sliding lid and the box from eachother.

3D Print of Second Iteration

Our second model was larger. We also printed out a few extra pieces including buttons and a small rectangle that would keep the display and battery pack in place. When changing the parameters of the box, the clearance did not change for some reason, so I had to sand the top in order for it to slide smoothly in place. Our buttons also required two iterations. Our second iteration had more accurate dimensions and accounted for the differences in the surrounding parts of the board for each button. Although our design worked, when looking at some other box designs in the classes, I wish we had ventured out a bit more with our original design to make something that wouldn't require additional pieces to keep the display and battery pack in place.

Lasercut Design in Fusion

Using Tim's demo in class and some help from Youtube we were able to make a box with finger-cut joints. Using tools like project, mirroring, and constraints, making the box and the finger-cut joints was not too difficult.

Laser Cut Iteration One

Our first iteration had a few issues. First, the finger-cut joints on the bottom piece did not cut. After looking back at Fusion, when making the sketch which I then exported as a DXF file, only the main body of the bottom piece and the etches for the long side pieces were selected and not the finger-cut joints for the short side pieces. Additionally, the distance between the cutout for the buttons and the bottom of that same long side piece was too small, which resulted in a finger-cut joint being entirely cut off.

Second Laser cut Iteration in Fusion

We did not change much for our second iteration. I only increased the distance between the cutout for the buttons and the bottom of the side piece. I also made sure the DXF files included all of the finger cut joints.

Second Laser cut Iteration

Each piece was able to fit together for our second iteration. I think that using glue or tape may be necessary to keep the box together without any additional support as the battery and display push on the long side pieces causing the entire box to collapse. This iteration definitely improved on the first iteration, but I think a few dimension changes would help keep everything tighter together. In the first two pictures (left to right), the orientation of the piece with the cutout is wrong and in the last two pictures (empty box and no batteries), the orientation is correct. The cutout for the buttons could be made a bit smaller to not show any other pieces of the electronic board.

Final Reflection

Overall, I really enjoyed this project. I enjoyed soldering even if I messed up my board a few times. I also really liked the partner aspect as it was a bit of a challenge as far as divvying up work and coordinating things, but it was nice to have someone who could help you. Also, learning to work with someone else on a singular project is something I feel like I've improved on through this project. I definitely feel more comfortable with Fusion and am excited to continue developing my modeling skills. However, I do wish that we made a more creative design that would eliminate the need for an extra piece to keep everything in place. Making the design for laser cutting was not too difficult despite having to think about how everything would work as 2D pieces mostly because we were only making a relatively simple box, but I can see how future projects that involve laser cutting may be harder to visualize. I am excited to laser cut more complex designs in the future.
