Project 5 Preliminary

Assistive Tech

Project Description:

This includes our scavenger hunt and initial research for project 5.

Scavenger Hunt

Design for Access

Evidence of design for navigation without one or more of the 5 senses

Our classroom number had braille, which aids the blind and visually impaired in making sure they have found the correct location. We noticed that braille was on most classroom numbers.

Evidence of design for forms of assistance

We found a handicapped button, which when pushed would open a door without the need to manually do it. This function is helpful for people who have difficulty opening doors.

Evidence of design for aging, broadly defined

An elevator can be incredibly helpful for aging folks, especially as their mobility begins to decrease. Unfortunately, the elevator in Ableson-Bass is pretty slow and a bit sketchy.

Evidence of design for young children

There are shorter urinals in most male bathrooms, which are helpful for young children who are usually on the shorter side.

Evidence of design for wheeled mobile gear

We found a ramp that was adjacent to some stairs. The ramp is much easier and safer to use for people in wheeled mobile gear compared to the stairs.

Design against Access

Evidence of design with physical barriers

Many of the labs, the biology labs in particular, require swipe access from authorized personnel to prevent any accidents or contimations.

Evidence of design with a lack of information

The bus stop by the Rabb steps does not have any information regarding routes and bus times. Additionally, there is no information about the TripShot app, which details the different bus and shuttle routes, their arrival times, and any other updates.

Evidence of design with an absence of help

Most of these emergency help callers/singalers on campus are not in use and I have never seen any repair being done on any of them.

Design Priorities in the Built Environment

Evidence of design for historical specificity

Usen Castle is in the only building of its kind on campus. It was built in 1928 and stood on the campus of Middlesex College of Medicine and Surgery until Brandeis acquired the land and the castle. The castle then served as a residence hall until 2016 and no longer any use per the Brandeis Hoot.

Brandeis Hoot Usen Castle
Evidence of design for environmental sustainability

The reuse containers are an example of environmental sustainability as their purpose is that students will continously reuse the same boxes and if they would rather use a disposable container, they pay a fee.

Evidence of design for health and well-being

Handsantizer stations are littered through campus and are more accessible and conveinent to use compared to going to the bathroom to wash your hands. In the wake of COVID, handsantizer stations are defintely more common, and for good reason.

Evidence of design for recreational use

There is a basketball hoop outside of Sherman dining hall, which students can use for recreational use.
