Ethnographic Interviews

Person 1

Person 1 is currently a freshman at Brandeis University. They are from the Boston area. Their most notable experiences with being left-handed were difficulties using a lecture hall chair desk when taking a test, smudging while writing, and having to use right-handed golf clubs when growing up.

Person 2

Person 2 is a junior at Brandeis University. They are from Littleton, Mass. They mostly discussed their troubles with smudging when writing with their left hand.

Person 3

Person 3 is a left-handed Professor at Brandies University. They grew up in New York City. They noted how growing up in a home with other left-handed family members made having access to left-handed tools such as scissors much easier. Additionally, they noted how despite being left-handed, they throw with their right hand. They also mentioned the common theme of difficulty writing and using lecture hall desks.

Person 4

Person 4 is a senior at Brandeis Univeristy. They are from Beijing, China. They mostly detailed their experience playing basketball as a left-handed player.

Person 5

Person 5 is a Brandeis alumn, who previously played on the Brandeis golf club team. They are from Malina and are right-handed. They mostly detailed their experience purchasing golf clubs and what types of clubs they preferred.

Story Board

Returning Back to How Might We Statements

After returning back to our How Might We Statements, we decided to focus on building empathy for left-handed people. We thought back to a few of our initial ideas that were too outlandish such as redesigning everything to be for lefties. For a more realistic approach, we thought about an escape room that involved using the left-handed versions of different tools, such as scissors or writing from right to left, to complete tasks in order to escape. For our prototype, we settled on making an "empathy kit" for left-handed people that included left-handed versions of a mouse and calipers as well as a left-handed writing tool. After this process and some discussion with Professor Krones, we are considering switching our goal to developing a universal golf club to altering a tool that would prevent smudging for left-handed writers.
